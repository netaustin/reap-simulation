ReEntry Resources

The movie 13TH, directed by Ava Duvernay, explores the connection between mass incarceration, racial discrimination, and the criminal legal system. It can be watched for free on YouTube.

Just Income GNV has another ReEntry Simulation which was developed by formerly incarcerated people, and which explores the impact of guaranteed income for the formerly incarcerated.

The Prison Policy Initiative maintains resources pertaining to many issues of mass incarceration.

The National Reentry Resource Center is the Department of Justice's source of information and guidance in reentry.

The Brennan Center for Justice has information on the social and economic harm of mass incarceration.

The Sentencing Project advocates for racial justice and sentencing reform.

The simulation was developed by Austin Smith during the ReEntry Acceleration Program at the Tamer Center for Social Enterprise at Columbia Business School.
